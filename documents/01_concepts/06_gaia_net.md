# Project 6: Gaia-Net

## Pillar
Productive / Deep Research

## The Core Concept
A continuous, autonomous climate and materials science "think tank." The system automatically ingests daily open-data feeds (e.g., global weather anomalies, new ArXiv materials science preprints, supply chain indices). A panel of specialized AI agents representing different disciplines (a "Climatologist," a "Materials Engineer," and a "Supply Chain Economist") analyzes this data to propose new hypothetical sustainable materials or climate resilience strategies, debate their viability, and publish synthesized "Briefing Papers" and "Feasibility Reports" as the final output.

## Why it Needs Iterative State
Genuine research is cumulative and cannot be fabricated in a single context window. The system maintains a localized "Knowledge Graph" of previously tested (and discarded) hypotheses, as well as an evolving "State of the Art" ledger. This ensures the agents don't repeat the same mistakes and can genuinely build upon theoretical breakthroughs they made three weeks ago.

## The Engine of Conflict/Discovery
The simulation is constantly perturbed by the injection of real-world data. If a real-world data feed indicates a sudden lithium supply shortage, the Economist agent will flag a critical alert, forcing the Materials Engineer agent to abandon its current 10-day research track and pivot toward alternative battery chemistries. This constant friction between theoretical research and stark real-world data constraints drives endless new avenues of inquiry.
