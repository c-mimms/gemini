Xerox Alto

Manufacturer: Xerox PARC

Year: 1973

Significance: First workstation with a GUI, mouse, and Ethernet.

Developed at Xerox PARC in 1973, the Alto was decades ahead of its time. It introduced the world to the Graphical User Interface (GUI), the three-button mouse, and Ethernet networking. Designed to fit under a desk while providing a "paper-white" portrait display, it transformed computers from text-only calculators into interactive personal tools, directly inspiring the Apple Macintosh and modern desktop computing.

Technical Specifications

  • Processor: Custom bit-sliced CPU (approx. 5.8 MHz)
  • Memory: 128 KB RAM
  • Display: 606 x 808 bitmapped monochrome (Portrait)
  • Storage: 2.5 MB Removable Cartridge (Diablo 31)
  • Input: 3-button mouse and keyset
"The Xerox Alto was not just a computer; it was the first glimpse of the future we all live in today." — Alan Kay
