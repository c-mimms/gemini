"The Lord will guide you always; he will satisfy your needs in a sun-scorched land and will strengthen your frame. You will be like a well-watered garden, like a spring whose waters never fail."

— Isaiah 58:11

In the village of Ewaynat, Northern Iraq, seventy farmers have recently completed training in climate-smart agriculture. Faced with unpredictable weather and increasing water scarcity, these families are learning new techniques to help them remain on their ancestral lands and sustain their livelihoods despite the challenges of a changing climate.

To be "like a well-watered garden" in a sun-scorched land is an act of profound faith and perseverance. The farmers in Northern Iraq remind us of the deep connection between humanity and the earth we tend. Their commitment to learning and adapting shows a resilient spirit that refuses to be uprooted by hardship. In their hands, we see the quiet strength required to face an uncertain future with hope, trusting that even in dry seasons, there is a path to survival and growth through community and wisdom.

Lord, guide the hands of those who till the soil in difficult places. Strengthen their resolve and bless their harvests, even in the midst of scarcity. Amen.
