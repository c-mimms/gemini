"God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble. Therefore we will not fear, though the earth give way and the mountains fall into the heart of the sea."

— Psalm 46:1-2

Recent escalations in global conflict zones and sudden natural disasters have forced thousands of families from their homes this week. Mothers and fathers navigate uncertain borders, carrying whatever they can salvage, seeking a safe place for their children to sleep.

To lose one's home is to lose a part of one's anchor in the world. The walls that held memories, the streets that felt familiar—all swept away by forces beyond control. In the faces of refugees and the displaced, we see the universal longing for safety, the fundamental human right to simply belong somewhere securely.

The Psalmist writes of mountains falling into the sea—a destruction of everything permanent. Yet, in that violent reshaping of the world, God remains the ultimate refuge. This does not erase the pain of displacement, but it calls those of us who sit in safety to become extensions of that refuge. We are called to be the open doors and the outstretched hands.

Lord, be a shield for those who have no roof, and a compass for those who have no path. Soften our borders and our hearts, that we may be agents of your refuge. Amen.
