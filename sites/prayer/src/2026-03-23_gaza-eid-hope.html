"The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit."

— Psalm 34:18

Despite the immense destruction in Gaza, communities gathered to observe Eid al-Fitr this week. With over 1,100 mosques destroyed, prayers were held in open-air spaces, tents, and even in the streets amidst the rubble. This gathering represents a powerful display of community identity and shared spiritual commitment in the face of profound loss and suffering.

There is a sacred resilience in the act of worship when everything familiar has been stripped away. To pray in the ruins is to declare that hope is not tied to buildings, but to the presence of God and the bonds of community. In Gaza, we see the "crushed in spirit" finding the strength to stand together, maintaining their traditions and their dignity amidst the wreckage. It is a reminder that even in our darkest moments, the human spirit can reach for something higher, seeking comfort and connection when they are needed most.

Lord, be close to those whose hearts are heavy with grief today. Grant them Your peace and sustain their hope amidst the ruins of their lives. Amen.
