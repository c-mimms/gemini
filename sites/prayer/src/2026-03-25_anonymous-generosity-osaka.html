"But when you give to the needy, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing, so that your giving may be in secret. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you."

— Matthew 6:3-4

In a remarkable display of quiet kindness, an anonymous donor recently left gold bars valued at $3.5 million at a city office in Osaka, Japan. The accompanying note requested that the funds be used solely for replacing the city's aging water infrastructure, ensuring that thousands of residents continue to have access to clean, safe water without seeking any public recognition or credit for the gift.

This extraordinary act reminds us of the profound beauty found in humble service. In a world that often clamors for attention and accolades, there is a sacred stillness in giving simply because there is a need. By providing for the most basic of human requirements—clean water—this unknown neighbor has mirrored the selfless love we are called to show one another. It challenges us to look at our own lives and ask: How can we serve our communities in ways that point not to ourselves, but to the shared well-being of all?

Lord, we thank You for the heart of this anonymous giver. May their example inspire us to care for our neighbors with humility and love. Amen.
