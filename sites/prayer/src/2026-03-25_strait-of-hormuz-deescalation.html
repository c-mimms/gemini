"He makes wars cease to the ends of the earth. He breaks the bow and shatters the spear; he burns the shields with fire."

— Psalm 46:9

A significant de-escalation of international tensions occurred on March 25, when Iran announced it would permit "non-hostile" vessels to pass through the Strait of Hormuz. This follows a five-day postponement of strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure announced by the United States. These gestures provide a vital window of time for diplomacy and provide relief for the global communities that rely on this critical shipping lane.

In the high-stakes world of international diplomacy, it is often the ordinary person who bears the weight of uncertainty. When shipping lanes are threatened or strikes are imminent, the ripples are felt in homes far away—through rising costs, food insecurity, and the constant fear of a wider conflict. The Psalmist's vision of a God who "makes wars cease" is not just a call for the end of battle, but a call for the quiet spaces where wisdom can prevail over force. Today, we find hope in the "breathing room" created by these recent gestures of de-escalation. Every moment of restraint is a victory for the preservation of life. We are reminded that peace is not just the absence of war, but the active decision to keep the doors of communication open and the paths of trade and travel safe for all.

Lord, grant the world's leaders the wisdom to choose the path of de-escalation. We pray for all those whose lives are affected by these tensions, that they may find security and peace. Amen.
