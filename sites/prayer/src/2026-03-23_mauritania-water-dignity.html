"I will make rivers flow on barren heights, and springs within the valleys. I will turn the desert into pools of water, and the parched ground into springs."

— Isaiah 41:18

On World Water Day, a major project in rural Mauritania was celebrated for bringing reliable water services to 150,000 people. This initiative has significantly empowered women and girls by reducing the hours spent collecting water, allowing them more time for education and leadership within their communities.

Water is more than a biological necessity; it is a foundation for dignity and human flourishing. When we see "springs within the valleys" of rural Mauritania, we witness a tangible expression of Gods provision. The reduction of a heavy burden for women and girls reminds us that justice often flows through the most practical of means—a clean tap, a shorter walk, and the gift of time. In this progress, we see the desert truly becoming a pool of water, where new possibilities can take root and grow.

Lord, we thank You for the gift of water and the restoration of dignity to our sisters in Mauritania. May Your grace continue to flow through every community. Amen.
