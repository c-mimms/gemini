"Carry each other’s burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ."

— Galatians 6:2

In Asheville, North Carolina, a community that has faced significant challenges following major storm events is now formalizing the power of its local connections. The city recently announced a $10 million investment to support "Resilience Hubs"—existing community spaces like local bookstores, churches, and neighborhood centers. These are the same places that served as lifelines during past crises, providing water, power, and reliable information. This investment aims to strengthen these hubs so they can serve as centers of support year-round and during any future challenges.

We often think of resilience as an individual strength, but true endurance is found in our relationships with one another. The "Resilience Hubs" of Asheville are beautiful, modern-day examples of what it means to carry one another's burdens. By building on the networks that naturally arise when we truly know and care for our neighbors, we create a community that is far more than the sum of its parts. It reminds us that our strength does not come from isolating ourselves, but from being deeply woven into the lives of those around us. In our shared vulnerability, we find a strength that cannot be shaken.

Lord, we thank You for the communities that care for one another. May we all find ways to be a "hub" of peace and support for those in our own neighborhoods today. Amen.
