"When the Lord restored the fortunes of Zion, we were like those who dreamed."

— Psalm 126:1

On March 24, Taliban authorities in Afghanistan released Dennis Coyle, an American linguist and researcher who had been detained for over a year. The release was described as a "humanitarian gesture" for the upcoming Eid al-Fitr holiday, facilitated by the diplomatic efforts of the UAE and Qatar. After long months of uncertainty, he is finally on his way home to his family.

There is a unique kind of joy that feels almost like a dream—the moment when a long-awaited door finally opens. The Psalmist speaks of a restoration so profound that it seemed surreal to those who experienced it. For Dennis Coyle and his loved ones, the past year has likely been a desert of waiting and worry. Yet, in the midst of complex international tensions, a simple humanitarian gesture has paved the way for a reunion. It reminds us that even in the most difficult circumstances, the human heart remains capable of mercy, and that no one is ever truly beyond the reach of hope. We celebrate this homecoming as a sign that the light of shared humanity can still pierce through the shadows of conflict.

Lord, we thank You for the safe release of Dennis Coyle. We pray for his peace as he returns home, and we ask that such gestures of mercy would multiply across the world. Amen.
