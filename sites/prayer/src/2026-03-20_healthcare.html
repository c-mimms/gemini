"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest." — Matthew 11:28

Around the world, hospitals and clinics face immense pressure as communities deal with seasonal outbreaks and ongoing health challenges. Medical professionals push through profound exhaustion, working long hours to care for the vulnerable, often at the expense of their own well-being.

When bodies fail and sickness spreads, the healers among us step forward into the fray. Their hands are the instruments of comfort; their presence is a reminder that we are not abandoned in our suffering. Yet, healers themselves break. They carry the weight of impossible decisions and the silent grief of losses they cannot perfectly prevent.

Christ's invitation is not a promise of an easy life, but of a resting place. It is a reminder that even those who carry the burdens of the world are allowed to lay them down. We recognize the profound humanity in our doctors, nurses, and caregivers, honoring their limits just as we honor their sacrifices.

Lord, grant rest to the weary healers. Strengthen their hands, guard their hearts, and let them feel the profound peace they so often give to others. Amen.
