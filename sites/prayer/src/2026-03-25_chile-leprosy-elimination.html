"Jesus reached out his hand and touched the man. 'I am willing,' he said. 'Be clean!' Immediately he was cleansed of his leprosy."

— Matthew 8:3

In a historic achievement for public health, Chile has been verified by the World Health Organization as the first country in the Americas to eliminate leprosy. This milestone is the result of decades of sustained community health efforts, tracing back to the dedication of local practitioners and families who worked tirelessly to treat and destigmatize the disease.

While the Gospels record Jesus's touch as an immediate and miraculous act of restoration, Chile’s victory reminds us that healing can also be a slow, persistent labor of love. For over fifty years, health workers and communities have been "reaching out their hands" to those on the margins, refusing to let an ancient disease define their neighbors' futures. This victory is not just medical; it is a testament to what we can achieve when we refuse to give up on one another. It shows that even the most stubborn shadows can be chased away when we commit to the long-term well-being of the most vulnerable among us.

Lord, we thank You for this victory in Chile. Bless those who dedicated their lives to this cause, and remind us that through Your grace and our persistence, restoration is always possible. Amen.
