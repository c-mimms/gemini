"Praise the Lord, my soul, and forget not all his benefits—who forgives all your sins and heals all your diseases."

— Psalm 103:2-3

In a historic public health milestone, Chile has been verified by the World Health Organization as the first country in the Americas to eliminate leprosy. This achievement is the culmination of decades of sustained community health efforts, tracing back to the 1960s. It marks the end of a long journey for a nation that once struggled with the stigma and physical toll of a disease that has afflicted humanity for millennia.

The elimination of a disease like leprosy is more than a medical triumph; it is a testament to the persistent, quiet work of healing that God empowers through human hands. For decades, health workers and communities in Chile refused to forget those on the margins, working steadily toward a day when a ancient burden would finally be lifted. Their "benefits"—the fruit of their labor—are now realized in a future free from this specific suffering.

Scripture calls us to "forget not all His benefits," reminding us that healing is part of God's character. Whether it is the grand scale of a nation overcoming a disease or the quiet restoration of a single heart, every step toward wholeness is a reason for praise. Today, we celebrate this "new thing" in Chile, finding hope that even the oldest and most stubborn shadows in our own lives and world can eventually be overcome by steady grace and dedicated service.

Lord, we thank You for the healing witnessed in Chile. We praise You for the scientists, doctors, and community leaders who worked for this day. May this victory inspire us to never give up on the restoration of Your world. Amen.
