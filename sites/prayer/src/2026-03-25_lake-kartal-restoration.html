"See, I am doing a new thing! Now it springs up; do you not perceive it? I am making a way in the wilderness and streams in the wasteland."

— Isaiah 43:19

Amidst the ongoing and heavy shadows of conflict, a small but powerful story of restoration has emerged from the Danube Delta in Ukraine. The rewilding of Lake Kartal, a multi-year effort to reconnect the river with the lake and restore its natural flow, has seen the ecosystem "bounce back" with incredible speed. Birds and fish have returned to the waters, proving that even in landscapes scarred by war, life persistently seeks a way to heal and flourish once more.

The resilience of nature often serves as a profound metaphor for the human spirit and our spiritual journeys. When we feel overwhelmed by the "wastelands" of life—be they external conflicts or internal struggles—it can be difficult to believe that renewal is possible. Yet, the waters of Lake Kartal remind us that the Creator is always "doing a new thing." This restoration is not just about the environment; it is a sign of hope that what has been broken can be mended, and what has been parched can be refreshed. We are invited to "perceive it" and to participate in the quiet, steady work of restoration in our own spheres of influence.

Lord, we praise You for the resilience of Your creation. We pray for all who live in regions of conflict, that they might see signs of Your healing work and find hope for a peaceful future. Amen.
