"The poor and needy search for water, but there is none; their tongues are parched with thirst. But I the Lord will answer them; I, the God of Israel, will not forsake them."

— Isaiah 41:17

As the African Union designates 2026 as the year for "Assuring Sustainable Water Availability," leaders highlight a stark reality: millions across the continent still lack access to clean water. AUC Chairperson Mahmoud Ali Youssouf reminded the world that for many, especially women and girls, the day begins with a miles-long walk to a water source. This fundamental "injustice" remains a daily struggle that underpins health, education, and human dignity.

The "long walk for water" is a journey of both physical exhaustion and profound resilience. It is a reminder of our most basic human needs and the deep inequalities that persist in our shared home. When we read that the "poor and needy search for water," it is not a distant metaphor; it is the lived experience of millions today. Their thirst is a cry that God hears, and His promise not to "forsake them" often finds its hands and feet in our collective response to global needs.

Water is life, both physically and spiritually. Just as parched earth waits for rain, our spirits often feel dry in the face of such massive global challenges. Yet, we are called to be the "answer" God promises—through advocacy, through sustainable development, and through an empathy that refuses to look away. We reflect today on how we might help clear the path for the "streams in the wasteland" to reach those who need them most.

Lord, we lift up those who walk miles today for a single bucket of water. We pray for the leaders of the African Union and all who work for sustainable solutions. Grant us hearts that thirst for justice as much as for water. Amen.
