Heritage & Horizon: The "Last Hurrah" Deep Dive

For this run, we’ve pivoted from generic scenic spots to experiences that specifically bridge her background as a pilot, her Swedish heritage, and the looming transition away from the Pacific Northwest. These are high-impact, low-crowd, and 100% baby-friendly.

Chris, please review these three distinct "vibes" for the Day-Of plan. Each addresses a different part of her personality. Which one feels most like "Her" for this final Seattle birthday?

Track A: The Day-Of Experience

Heritage & Water

The Little Norway Cruise

A private morning in Poulsbo. Rent an enclosed, quiet Duffy Electric Boat for a slow cruise of Liberty Bay. It’s warm, baby-safe (no engine noise), and offers a direct link to her Scandinavian roots.

Enclosed cabin, easy boarding, life jackets for infants provided.
Nature & Peace

The Island Sanctuary

A retreat to Bainbridge Island for the Bloedel Reserve. 150 acres of world-class gardens with a main loop that is fully stroller-accessible. Followed by a high-end lunch at Ba Sa or Hitchcock.

The quintessential PNW garden experience before leaving the region.
Aviation & Views

The Horizon View

A 20-minute Kenmore Air Scenic Tour departing from Lake Union. As a pilot, she’ll appreciate the DHC-2 Beaver’s handling and the bird’s-eye view of the city she’s about to leave behind.

Aviation background meets "Last Hurrah" sentimentality. 1-year-olds fly free on lap.

Track B: The Gift Hunt

Beyond the day-of, we’ve sourced five gifts that range from physical luxury to deeply sentimental projects.

1. Georg Jensen "Daisy" Collection

Iconic Swedish/Danish luxury jewelry. The Daisy necklace is feminine, timeless, and a direct nod to her heritage. It’s an "investment" piece she’ll keep forever.

2. The "Captain’s Log" Sentimental Project

A custom-bound, high-end leather journal formatted like a pilot’s logbook, but filled with photos and memories of their daughter’s first year in the PNW. A physical time-capsule of this chapter.

3. Svenskt Tenn Pewter Lion

The pinnacle of Stockholm interior design. A small, heavy pewter lion (or heart) from the legendary Strandvägen store. It’s a classic "Swedish Home" staple.

4. "Last Hurrah" Family Session

A professional photoshoot at Discovery Park or Gas Works. Capturing the family in their Seattle element one last time before the move in July.

5. Filson Rugged Twill Tote

The ultimate PNW luxury staple. Durable, stylish, and perfect for the upcoming move and future travels. Local craftsmanship at its best.

Does she already have high-end aviation gear (like Bose A30s)? If not, that’s a potential "splurge" gift, but it might feel too much like "work" equipment.
