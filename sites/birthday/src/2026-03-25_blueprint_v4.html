The Strategy Shift: From Ideas to Archetypes

After a deep dive into logistics (specifically for the 1-year-old) and her core interests, I've narrowed down the day-trip options into three distinct "Archetypes." Each balances the "PNW Last Hurrah" with her background in aviation, heritage, and nautical life.

Decision Needed: Which of these three vibes feels most like her? We need to lock in the "Vibe" so I can begin vetting specific reservations and backup plans for April 12.

Aviation Focus

The Sky & Sea Scenic

A 20-minute Kenmore Air scenic flight departing from Kirkland or Lake Union. Lap infants (under 2) are welcome! Followed by a waterfront lunch in Kirkland.

Connects her pilot background with a quintessential Seattle experience.
Nautical Focus

The Kirkland Cruise

A private, near-silent Duffy electric boat rental in Kirkland. Bring a high-end Swedish picnic. The boat is fully enclosed and heated if needed—perfect for the baby.

The quietest and most stable water option for a 1-year-old.
Bloom Focus

The Skagit Splendor

A drive to the Skagit Valley Tulip Festival. April 12 is peak bloom. We'll need to use a baby carrier (muddy fields!), but it's the most iconic "PNW Spring" moment.

A must-do before leaving the PNW, and very visually stunning.
