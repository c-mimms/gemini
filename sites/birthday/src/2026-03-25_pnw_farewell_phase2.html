PNW Farewell: The Final Chapters (Phase 2)

Building on our initial leads, here are three entirely new, diverse directions for the day and the gift. These focus on privacy, her pilot background, and the impending move.

"The Private Sound" (Lake Washington Luxury)

The Vibe: Private, exclusive, and serene. Rent a luxury electric Duffy boat from Renton or South Lake Union. Pack a high-end brunch or have it catered.

Baby Logic: Extremely safe and quiet. The baby can nap on the boat while you cruise the shoreline. No crowds to navigate.

"Garden Grandeur" (Bainbridge & Bloedel)

The Vibe: Sophisticated, natural, and calm. Take the ferry (a Seattle iconic experience) to Bainbridge. Spend the morning at Bloedel Reserve’s world-class forest gardens.

Baby Logic: Bloedel’s main loop is stroller-accessible. Winslow offers many high-end, family-friendly dining options (e.g., Ba Sa).

"Aviation & Al Fresco" (Mukilteo/Everett)

The Vibe: A tribute to her background as a pilot. Visit the Boeing Future of Flight (Mukilteo) for the latest in aviation. Follow with a waterfront picnic at the lighthouse.

Baby Logic: The museum is spacious and stroller-friendly. Mukilteo waterfront is accessible and scenic.

"The Gift Hunt: Phase 2"

1. "The Sky Above" Custom Art: A high-end aviation map print or custom wood carving of her favorite flying route or the BFI/SEA airspace. A permanent piece of her career to take with her.

2. "The PNW Patina" (Filson): A classic piece of Seattle craftsmanship—perhaps a rugged twill luggage bag or tote for her upcoming move.

3. "Nordic Nuance" (Georg Jensen): High-end Danish/Swedish design (jewelry or home decor) from a boutique like Woodland Mod, connecting her heritage to the move.
