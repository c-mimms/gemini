PNW Farewell: The Final Chapters

With only 4 months left in Seattle, these leads focus on iconic Pacific Northwest experiences that are baby-friendly but prioritize your wife's enjoyment of this unique region.

Whidbey Island Escape

The Vibe: A seamless island day-trip. Ferry from Mukilteo to Clinton, followed by a visit to the quaint town of Langley. Lunch at The Inn at Langley offers high-end dining with sweeping views.

Baby Logic: Extremely stroller-friendly streets in Langley. Double Bluff Beach provides a massive sandy expanse for a crawler to explore safely.

Salish Lodge & Snoqualmie Falls

The Vibe: Iconic PNW luxury. A high-end brunch overlooking the falls. It's powerful, scenic, and feels distinctly Washington.

Baby Logic: The falls viewing platforms are all stroller-accessible. The lodge is accustomed to high-end family service.

Skagit Valley Tulip Festival

The Vibe: April 12th is peak bloom. A visual masterpiece. We recommend Tulip Town specifically for its easier logistics and indoor displays.

Baby Logic: Use a carrier for the fields, but keep the stroller in the car for the paved display areas and cafe.

The "Heritage & Horizon" Gift

Idea 1: Glass Art with Mt. St. Helens Ash. A hand-blown piece from Glass Eye Studio. It incorporates actual ash from the 1980 eruption — a literal piece of PNW history to take to your next chapter.

Idea 2: Woodland Mod Selection. A high-end piece of Swedish-inspired modern design from the Ballard-based boutique. Bridges her heritage with her time in Seattle.
