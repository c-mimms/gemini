Scenic & Travel

A quintessential Seattle experience that perfectly balances industrial fascination with botanical beauty. The Hiram M. Chittenden Locks (commonly known as the Ballard Locks) provide a unique spectacle as boats transit between the freshwater of Lake Washington and the saltwater of Puget Sound. The surrounding Carl S. English Jr. Botanical Garden offers a lush, quiet escape for a leisurely walk among rare trees and flower beds.

Baby Factor: Extremely stroller-friendly with wide, paved paths. The fish ladder and the slow, rhythmic movement of boats provide gentle visual stimulation for a one-year-old without overwhelming noise.
