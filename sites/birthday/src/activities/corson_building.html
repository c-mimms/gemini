Dining

Family-Style Elegance in Georgetown

The Corson Building is one of Seattle’s most special dining destinations. Set in a 1910-built home in Georgetown, it offers a "family-style" fine dining experience that is both sophisticated and grounded. The outdoor courtyard and intimate indoor spaces feel more like a private dinner party than a restaurant, making it surprisingly comfortable for a one-year-old to be part of a high-end celebration.

Baby Factor: The communal/family-style vibe and outdoor garden space make it less restrictive than traditional white-tablecloth fine dining. Early seatings are recommended.
