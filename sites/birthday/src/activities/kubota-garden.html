Exploration

A hidden gem in South Seattle, the Kubota Garden is a stunning 20-acre landscape that blends Japanese garden design with native Northwest plants. Founded by Fujitaro Kubota in 1927, it offers a more expansive and varied experience than the traditional Japanese Garden in the Arboretum. With its winding paths, peaceful ponds, and iconic red bridges, it is a world-class aesthetic escape that feels like a discovery. It is exceptionally stroller-friendly on the main paths, offering a serene, high-end nature walk for the whole family.

Baby Factor: Very high. The wide, paved or packed-gravel paths are manageable for a stroller, and the scale of the garden means it never feels crowded, providing a quiet environment for the baby.
